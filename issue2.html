Inside the Algorithm: What’s Really Playing in Your Focus Playlist

This week, we’re shining a light on something that’s been lurking quietly in Spotify’s world of “focus” playlists—those chilled, lo-fi, deep house-adjacent soundtracks meant for studying, working, or zoning out. While they may seem harmless enough, many of the so-called artists behind these tracks don’t exist in any meaningful way.


As reported by Liz Pelly in Harper’s Magazine, these playlists are increasingly populated by ghost artists—names without faces, gigs, or communities—created by production farms or, in some cases, quietly commissioned by Spotify itself. The goal? Maximize streams while minimizing royalty payouts.


These tracks, known internally as “Perfectly Functional Content,” are engineered for background listening. But that efficiency comes at a cost: authenticity. Real producers and DJs—especially those from underground and independent scenes—are pushed further out of the frame by algorithmic filler.

Infinite Mixes, Real Energy

But here’s the good news: the underground never left.


If you want music curated by actual people—DJs, selectors, diggers, and heads—tune into NTS Radio, Resonance FM, or The Lot Radio. These platforms offer infinite mixes, live sets, radio shows, and archives that capture the depth, texture, and context the algorithm can’t touch.


NTS in particular is a goldmine: from hypnotic deep house to experimental electronics, global grooves, ambient zones, and sweaty club nights, it’s all there—broadcast live or archived for whenever you’re ready to tune in. Want deep focus? Try In Focus. Need a sunrise come-down set? Dig through The Early Bird Show, Kristina Records, or Soup To Nuts.


This isn’t passive listening—it’s cultural connection.


So if you’re feeling the fatigue of playlist monoculture, maybe it’s time to swap autoplay for exploration. There’s a whole world of infinite mixes waiting—real music, real selectors, no ghosts.
